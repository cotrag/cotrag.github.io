I am a quantitative social science researcher with experience in economic consulting and political science research. My work spans consumer lending data and cross-national survey research, with a focus on producing clear, reproducible analysis for a wide range of audiences.

## Skills

**Statistical methods:** Causal inference, econometrics, regression modeling, survey methodology, data validation, data visualization

**Programming:** R, Python, Stata, SQL

**Tools:** ArcGIS, SPSS, LaTeX

## Experience

**Graduate Research Assistant**, Comparative National Elections Project, Ohio State University (2024–present)
- Validate and integrate survey data from international field researchers into a longitudinal cross-national dataset
- Develop coding protocols and data documentation used by a distributed team of international researchers
- Execute statistical analyses in R applying causal inference techniques to electoral behavior and voter attitudes
- Translate findings into data visualizations and presentations for academic seminars and conferences

**Analyst / Associate, Financial Economics Practice**, Charles River Associates (2021–2023)
- Built Stata and Python data pipelines to clean and analyze large-scale consumer lending datasets, including HMDA-reportable mortgage records and auto financing data
- Produced result tables and written reports translating econometric findings for clients, opposing counsel, and federal regulatory bodies
- Managed 4–5 simultaneous litigation support projects under strict deadlines
- Co-authored a thought leadership memo analyzing the effect of COVID-era debt management programs on credit card default rates

**Research Assistant**, UBI Center (2021)
- Developed Python-based economic analyses of universal basic income policies, modeling distributional effects on the racial wealth gap for a published report
- Collaborated with an international research team to design and run policy simulations
